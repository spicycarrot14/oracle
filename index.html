1. Purpose

This Concept of Operations (CONOPS) establishes procedures and responsibilities for the 18th Space Squadron (18 SQ) in conducting Space Domain Awareness (SDA) proximity operations and coordinating near-field activities for assigned orbital assets. It defines operator roles, escalation thresholds, and autonomous authority boundaries applicable to all active proximity approaches within assigned orbital regimes.

2. Background

The low Earth orbit (LEO) environment presents increasing operational complexity due to a growing population of active and defunct resident space objects (RSOs), active maneuvers by non-cooperative RSOs without prior notification, expanding debris populations from prior fragmentation events, and variable space weather effects on orbital mechanics and communications. The 18 SQ operates under USSPACECOM authority with persistent awareness responsibilities across assigned orbital regimes.

3. Mission

The 18 SQ will provide persistent, accurate space domain awareness to detect, characterize, and respond to anomalous RSO activity; monitor and mitigate space weather effects on assigned assets; coordinate launch detection correlation; and ensure safe approach corridors for all authorized proximity operations within assigned orbital regimes.

4. Execution

The following sub-sections define execution standards for each operational domain. Operators will apply these procedures in conjunction with real-time ORACLE intelligence and APEX autonomous execution protocols. All decisions and approvals will be logged with operator identification and timestamp.

4.1   Approach Corridor Management

Proximity operations will be conducted within designated approach corridors per IADC proximity operations guidelines. Operators will maintain minimum 1 km safety margins for all LEO proximity operations. Conjunction probability (Pc) assessments exceeding 1/1,000 will trigger mandatory maneuver review and chain of command notification. Corridor boundaries will be reassessed following any observed RSO maneuver within the protected zone. Operators may not proceed within the inner keep-out zone without explicit L3 authorization.

4.2   Conjunction Assessment Procedures

Conjunction screening will be conducted using Space-Track catalog data refreshed at minimum 4-hour intervals. When a close approach event (CAE) is detected within 7 days, operators will initiate a conjunction data message (CDM) review cycle. Escalation thresholds are as follows: Pc > 1/10,000 (L1 Watch), Pc > 1/1,000 (L2 Operator Review), Pc > 1/100 (L3 Maneuver Authority Required). All conjunction events will be cross-referenced with WARDEN DEV scoring prior to maneuver execution.

4.3   Space Weather Monitoring

All proximity operations must account for solar activity. Enhanced atmospheric drag during elevated geomagnetic conditions (Kp ≥ 5) will alter predicted trajectories and must be incorporated into all maneuver planning. HF radio blackouts during X-class solar flare events will degrade ground-to-vehicle communications; ODC autonomous operations are authorized to continue within approved intent envelopes during these intervals. Operations shall be suspended or replanned when solar proton events exceed established threshold limits. TLE refresh intervals will be reduced to 2 hours during G2+ storm conditions.

4.4   RSO Tracking and Characterization

Non-cooperative RSOs operating within 50 km of assigned assets will receive enhanced tracking via all available sensors. Uncharacterized propulsive maneuvers will trigger a DEViation (DEV) score assessment per WARDEN thresholds within 30 minutes of detection. RSOs with confirmed burn sequences without corresponding maneuver plans will be escalated for intelligence review and correlated against known inspection satellite profiles maintained in the Space-Track catalog.

4.5   Debris Avoidance

Active tracking of catalogued debris populations is mandatory for all proximity operations. Legacy high-density clusters from prior fragmentation events (e.g., Fengyun-1C series, 2007-010) require dedicated conjunction screening overlays beyond standard catalog processing. Fragment cloud modeling will be updated following each sensor pass that adds new objects to the catalog. Aggregate conjunction risk elevation above nominal will trigger enhanced screening intervals and mandatory operator notification.

4.6   Communication Windows and Sensor Health

Communication windows and ground station availability will be pre-planned and published 24 hours in advance. Sensor health degradation events affecting mission-critical coverage will be immediately escalated and redundant communication paths activated. ODC autonomous operations may continue during planned blackout windows within operator-approved APEX intent envelopes. Post-blackout audit logs will be reviewed within 60 seconds of link restoration.

4.7   Launch Coordination and Detection

All domestic launches will be coordinated 48 hours in advance with range safety authorities. SBIRS-based launch detection events will be cross-referenced against scheduled launches within 2 minutes of plume detection. Unscheduled launches or launches from non-cooperative parties will trigger ORACLE alert protocols and WARDEN monitoring thresholds. TEL stand-down confirmation is required within 30 minutes of all observed launches.

5. Command and Control

The 18 SQ Commander retains operational authority for all proximity operations. ORACLE provides the authoritative intelligence picture for operator decision-making. APEX ODC provides autonomous execution within operator-approved intent envelopes. All escalation decisions will be logged with operator ID and timestamp. The three-tier control architecture (Ground Policy → ODC Execution → Vehicle Autonomy) defines authority boundaries for all mission phases.

6. Risk Management

All operations will maintain fuel reserves above minimum thresholds per APEX Intent Envelope parameters. Post-operation reviews will assess ODC decision accuracy and refine autonomous authority bounds over time. The following sub-sections address domain-specific risk factors.

6.2   Space Weather Risk

Operations during G2 or greater geomagnetic storm conditions require senior operator approval. LEO trajectory models will be updated every 4 hours during elevated Kp conditions. GPS-dependent operations will carry manual backup procedures during all active space weather watch periods. Operators will monitor NOAA SWPC forecasts and escalate via ORACLE when watch thresholds are breached.

6.3   Autonomous Operations Risk

ODC autonomous decision authority is bounded by the operator-approved intent envelope at all times. All autonomous decisions are logged with full context, confidence scores, and sensor fusion provenance for post-operation review. Operators retain override authority throughout all mission phases. Abort triggers are hardware-enforced and cannot be disabled by software command.
